Coconut Shell Charcoal Briquette

Coconut charcoal briquettes are one of the processed products from coconut shells that produce environmentally friendly products. This briquette product has many benefits and is safe for use in daily activities.

In addition to being an eco-friendly product, coconut charcoal briquettes also serve as an alternative energy source to petroleum. Made from natural materials, these products are recyclable and help reduce waste.

High-quality coconut shell charcoal has a specific composition. Its water content is about 6.24%, which indicates a low level of moisture and makes it more effective when burned. The volatile matter in it is 5.46%, which means few components will vaporize when the charcoal is heated, thus reducing smoke and improving combustion efficiency. The ash content of this charcoal is only 0.54%, indicating that the residue after combustion is minimal, which is a good indicator of charcoal quality. Finally, the charcoal has a fixed carbon content of 87.76%, which is the main element that provides heat energy. The higher the fixed carbon content, the longer and more stable the charcoal burns. With this composition, coconut shell charcoal is an ideal choice for various purposes, including cooking and industrial use.

Coco Yarns/Ropes

Coconut rope or yarn is a natural fiber product derived from coconut fiber, often referred to as coir. This fiber is obtained from the hard outer shell of the coconut fruit. Coconut rope has many uses due to its strength and eco-friendly nature, making it ideal for applications in areas such as agriculture, handicrafts, and the maritime industry.

The process of making coconut rope begins with the removal of fibers from the outer part of the coconut shell, known as the mesocarp. This process can be done manually or using a machine. Once taken, the fibers are dried to reduce their moisture content. The dried fibers are then spun into yarn or rope by twisting or twisting them to form strong strands. Multiple strands of yarn can be combined to make a thicker and stronger rope. In some cases, this drying and spinning process can be improved with modern technology to produce high-quality coconut rope.

Usability

  • Agriculture and Plantation: Used to tie up crops, support weak stems, or as mulch material.
  • Maritime Industry: Used as rope on ships as it is resistant to saltwater.
  • Handicrafts: Used in the manufacture of various craft products such as bags, carpets, and wall hangings.
  • Building and Construction: Used as straps or in the manufacture of traditional scaffolding.
